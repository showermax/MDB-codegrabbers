What is a codegrabbing

January 31, 2023 by KGB

Codegrabbing is a technique used to intercept and decode the signal sent between a keyless entry system and the car.

The process involves using a device known as a codegrabber, which is designed to listen in on the communication between the key and the car, and then capture the code that is sent.

Codegrabbers come in different shapes and sizes, but they all work on the same principle. They contain a radio transmitter and receiver, which are used to pick up the signals sent by the car and the key. When a codegrabber intercepts a signal, it records the code that is sent and stores it in its memory. Once the code has been captured, the codegrabber can be used to replay the signal, effectively opening the car without a key.

Codegrabbers are often used by criminals to steal cars, but they can also be used for more benign purposes. For example, if a car owner loses their key or accidentally locks it inside their car, a locksmith or car technician can use a codegrabber to decode the lock and gain access to the vehicle. This can be a faster and more cost-effective solution than having to replace the entire lock or keyless entry system.

However, it is important to note that using a codegrabber to open a car without a key is illegal unless you are a licensed locksmith or car technician. Attempting to use a codegrabber to steal a car is a serious crime and can result in severe legal consequences. To protect against codegrabbing, car owners should take precautions such as keeping their keys in a signal-blocking pouch or box when not in use, parking their cars in secure areas, and installing additional security features such as alarms and immobilizers.

In conclusion, codegrabbing is a technique used by criminals to intercept and decode the signal sent between a keyless entry system and the car. While codegrabbers can be used for legitimate purposes such as helping car owners who have lost their keys, they can also be used for illegal activities. Car owners should take steps to protect against codegrabbing and ensure that only licensed professionals use codegrabbers to access their vehicles.
